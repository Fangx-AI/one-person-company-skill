# AI Commerce Visuals

Purpose: help product judgment distinguish consumer AI image play from merchant-paid commerce-material workflows.

## Virtual Try-On / Product-On-Model References

### direct competitors

- FASHN AI: virtual try-on API. Commercial value sits in model image + garment image generation, e-commerce latency, API integration, and image credits.
- VModel: virtual try-on API and AI fashion model workflow. Value sits in product-on-model generation for sellers, usage-based pricing, and lower shooting cost.
- Botika: AI fashion model and product photo workflow for fashion brands. Value sits in model diversity, generated product photos, HD output, and commerce-ready visuals.
- VTry: virtual try-on and fashion visual workflow. Value sits in imported garments, high-resolution output, team material library, and subscription/credits.

### adjacent substitutes

- 真人模特拍摄 and studio product photography.
- P 图修图师, e-commerce designers, detail-page design services.
- 小红书/抖音种草图生产, buyer-show collection, live-commerce screenshots.
- Generic AI image tools that can generate fashion scenes but do not preserve product fidelity.

### free substitutes

- Open-source virtual try-on models, ComfyUI workflows, Stable Diffusion workflows, and image-editing scripts.
- Manual product flat lays, mannequin photos, merchant selfies, buyer-show reuse.
- Generic image generation prompts without SKU fidelity.

### high-price alternatives

- Fashion photography studios.
- E-commerce visual outsourcing and agency retainers.
- Brand-owned content production teams.
- Enterprise API, private deployment, SLA, security review, and batch pipeline integration.

## payment mechanism

Common monetization routes:

- API credits per generated image.
- Monthly subscription with image quota.
- HD / 4K / watermark removal upsell.
- Batch generation and team material library.
- Private deployment, SLA, invoice, security, and support.
- commerce-material service: seller gives garment images, founder delivers usable product-on-model images, Xiaohongshu covers, detail-page images, or ad creatives.

## One-Person Company Wedge

Avoid starting with a broad C-end dress-up app. The first paid wedge should be a narrow B-end workflow:

- Xiaohongshu fashion sellers need weekly product-on-model images.
- Taobao / Douyin small shops need cheap detail-page and seeding visuals.
- Private-domain group sellers need fast product story images.
- Small brands need test creatives before paying for real shoots.

First offer:

```text
Seller gives 5 garment images.
You deliver 20 product-on-model commerce images.
Price: 99 / 199 / 499 RMB depending on count, HD, and revision boundary.
```

## Judgment Rule

Do not judge the idea by whether image generation is technically impressive. Judge by:

- buyer: fashion seller, e-commerce operator, agency, brand, or consumer
- paid job: product-on-model image, SKU visual test, Xiaohongshu seeding image, detail-page material, ad creative
- quality risk: garment fidelity, body pose, occlusion, texture, face, and unrealistic try-on
- cost risk: API cost per playful consumer generation
- rights risk: portrait rights, product image rights, platform moderation, and misleading ad claims
- delivery fit: can one person manually serve the first 10 sellers before building a full app

## Stop-Loss

Use these stop-loss rules before building a full app:

Stop or narrow when:

- 10 target sellers produce zero willingness to pay 99 RMB for a sample package.
- Merchants only want free novelty images and will not provide real SKU images.
- Output quality requires heavy manual retouching for every order.
- API cost or failed generations make gross margin impossible below 199 RMB.
- Platform or ad-use risk prevents merchants from publishing the generated images.
