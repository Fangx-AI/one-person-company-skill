# Business Judgment

## First Principle

A product idea is not a business until a reachable user pays for a specific outcome often enough to cover acquisition, delivery, maintenance, and founder time.

## Product Judgment Rubric

Score the idea on five gates:

1. **Paid pain**: Is the user already spending money, time, reputation, or operational attention on this workflow?
2. **Payment moment**: Why would they pay now instead of saving the tool for later?
3. **Reachability**: Can a solo founder reach the first 20 buyers without paid ads or broad brand building?
4. **Delivery leverage**: Does each sale create low incremental work, or does it become disguised freelancing?
5. **Market proof**: Are there comparable products, agencies, templates, plugins, courses, newsletters, communities, or substitute workflows that show budget exists?

If two or more gates fail, call the idea weak. Give one salvage path only if the paid workflow can be narrowed.

## Useful Lenses

- Function is not the same as business. "Markdown to HTML" is a function; paid business may be API credits, batch conversion, compliance-safe publishing, team workflow, or CMS integration.
- A tiny tool can be viable when the buyer has repeated urgency, switching cost, or professional consequences.
- Consumer curiosity is usually not payment intent. B2B workflow friction, revenue generation, risk reduction, and status-sensitive outcomes monetize better.
- For one-person companies, the best wedge is often a painful niche workflow plus direct access to users, not a large abstract market.

## Output Shape

Use this structure for `/product` or `/产品判断`:

```text
判断：能做 / 谨慎做 / 不建议做

为什么：
- 用户是谁，付费场景是什么
- 市面上谁已经在收钱，靠什么收
- 这个想法商业上最硬的一点
- 最危险的一点

一人公司路径：
- 第一个可卖版本
- 第一批 20 个用户从哪里来
- 价格锚点

7 天验证：
- 今天做什么
- 合格标准
- 停损线
```

Keep the final action small enough to complete today, but not so small that it avoids payment validation.
